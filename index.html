Morning Commentary · Wednesday 4 March 2026
The dominant story remains the US–Israel–Iran conflict (Operation Epic Fury, launched Feb 28). Iran confirmed the Strait of Hormuz is closed to commercial shipping; the IRGC threatened to attack any vessel attempting transit. Roughly 20% of global seaborne oil passes through the strait. Markets are caught between the energy inflation shock and Trump's pledge to escort tankers, which trimmed Tuesday's session lows. The S&P 500 fell as much as 2.5% before recovering to close −0.94%; the Dow −0.83%; Nasdaq −1.02%. Asian markets extend losses Wednesday — Nikkei −4%, KOSPI −7% (largest since 2008). Bonds are not providing a safe haven: the US 10Y yield rose +7bp to 4.10% on inflation re-pricing; the "buy bonds in a crisis" playbook is broken. Gold pulled back from its $5,350+ peak to ~$5,075–5,155 but structural demand remains intact. WTI settled +4.68% to $74.56; Brent +4.71% to $81.40 — both off intraday highs after Trump's tanker-escort statement. Natural gas +30% on Qatar LNG export halt. Key watch today: any Iran–US diplomatic signal, OPEC+ production response, Friday NFP.
