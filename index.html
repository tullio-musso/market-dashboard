Morning Commentary · Sunday 29 Mar 2026
Markets close out a bruising March on a downbeat note. The S&P 500 fell 1.67% Friday to 6,368.85 — its fifth consecutive weekly decline and lowest close since September 2025 — bringing the month's loss to 6.8%, the worst since December 2022. The Nasdaq Composite dropped 2.15% to 20,948 and has now fallen more than 13% from its October record, firmly in correction territory. The dominant force remains the US–Iran war (now in its fifth week): Brent crude closed at $112.57 on Saturday, WTI at $99.64 — both up 50%+ YTD — stoking inflation fears that have pushed Fed rate-hike probability above 50% for the first time this cycle. US 10Y yields hit 4.42%, an eight-month high. The week's most notable development outside energy: SpaceX filed confidentially for IPO on Thursday, targeting a raise of $75B+ that would value the company at ~$1.5 trillion.
